The Problem

Food–Drug Interactions Are Trapped in Prose

FDA labeling rules (21 CFR 201.57) explicitly require clinically significant food interactions with practical management instructions — yet this critical safety data remains locked in unstructured text, fragmented across label sections, and impossible to query at scale.

📄

Narrative, Not Computable

Food-interaction data lives in free-text paragraphs across Drug Interactions, Dosage & Administration, and Clinical Pharmacology sections. No existing database (DrugBank, KEGG, ChEMBL, SIDER) structures food–drug interactions specifically.

🔀

Sectional Fragmentation

FDA guidance links "Effect of Food" in Clinical Pharmacology to Dosage & Administration instructions — but labels can state conflicting food-effect instructions in different sections, with no automated way to detect discrepancies.

⚠️

Labeling ≠ FDA-Approved

DailyMed warns its "in use" labeling may not match FDA-approved labeling and may not be verified by FDA. openFDA cautions not to rely on results for medical decisions. FDALabel notes SPL may differ from approved labeling at Drugs@FDA.

🤖

AI Hallucination Risk

LLM-based extraction adds confabulation risk. NIST's Generative AI Profile flags content provenance as a key concern. Fabricated citations have been documented in medical LLM outputs. Safety demands retrieval-grounded extraction only.

Why It Matters

High-Impact Interactions Hiding in Labels

These clinically important food–drug interactions demonstrate why structured, computable extraction is urgently needed. The same food can increase or decrease drug exposure depending on mechanism.

The Solution

Provenance-Locked Structured Claims

Every food–drug interaction claim in the FDI Commons links to the exact label snippet, SPL version, effective date, and LOINC section code — so you always know where a claim comes from, what evidence tier it represents, and whether it's current.

01

Mandatory Provenance

Source label, SPL set ID, effective date, LOINC section code, character offsets, and snippet hash — never optional. Every claim traceable to exact text.

02

FDI Claim Schema

Drug (RxNorm), food trigger, interaction type (PK/PD/food-effect), directionality, action instruction, timing qualifiers, mechanism, and evidence tier (label-stated / human study / mechanistic).

03

Zero-Hallucination Policy

LLM use is optional and strictly retrieval-grounded (RAG). Citations are programmatically attached from retrieved snippets — never LLM-generated. Automated citation verification on every claim.

04

Evidence Tier Separation

Label-stated: from regulatory text. Human clinical: peer-reviewed evidence. Mechanistic-only: in vitro / plausible mechanism. Tiers never auto-upgrade.
